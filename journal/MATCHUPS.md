## vs ENNEACRAFT  — `STATUS: CONFIRMED 2026-06-14 (md-matchup-analyst deep pass)`
Opp role: **T3 power (4.5) but #2 most-played (10.18%)** — you face it constantly.
**ID confidence ~90%:** "enneatech / ennea-something" → **Enneacraft** ("ennea-" = Greek *nine*; wins by banking **9 E.C. Counters** → 900 burn each). "Ennead" ruled out — no such archetype (ygoprodeck `?name=Ennead` → HTTP 400). Confirm with the user: *"face-down monsters that flip up to negate your plays and burn you out over several turns — that's the deck wrecking you?"*

**What it does (why it feels miserable):** a true **control** deck — no turn-1 combo. Monsters sit **face-down in Defense** and **flip up in response to what YOU do**, applying a FLIP disruption (negate / banish-from-hand / destroy), banking counters. Field Spell **Enneapolis** removes all counters in the End Phase for 900 damage each (9 = lethal). It extends the game to turn 4+ and makes *your* turn fall apart.

**Key threats (verbatim):**
- **Deftero Enneacraft "alazoneIA"** (starter, Lv1 EARTH): *FLIP: banish 1 random card from opp hand until End Phase; when opp activates an effect that adds from Deck to hand (Quick Effect): flip up, add 1 "Enneacraft" from Deck. Reveal in hand: SS 1 monster from hand face-down in Defense...*
- **Enneacraft - Atil.SPIA** (negate boss, Lv9 LIGHT 3000): after flipped, *opponent cannot activate cards/effects in response to their own activation*; at **Chain Link 3+** flips up to **negate + destroy** your chain.
- **Enneacraft - Asta.PIXEA** (anti-target boss, Lv9 LIGHT 3000): *opponent cannot target cards on field/GY*; when you **target** a card it controls, flips up to **negate that effect AND banish a random card from your hand.**
- **Enneapolis** (Field): End Phase, remove all counters → 900 damage each.

**What lands vs you / what whiffs:**
| Your card | Lands? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Ash Blossom** | **LANDS (best)** | Deftero's "reveal → add 1 Enneacraft from Deck" is a deck-to-hand add — Ash chokes their consistency. |
| **Droll & Lock Bird** | STRONG (you don't run it, but it's the tech answer) | The whole deck chains off "add from Deck" — Droll after their first add shuts the engine. Tune candidate vs an Enneacraft-heavy ladder. |
| **Maxx "C"** | situational | They're low-summon control and run their own Maxx "C"; taxes but draws less than vs combo. |
| **Infinite Impermanence** | **DEAD game 1** | CORRECTION (2026-06-14): Imperm needs a **face-up** monster; their board is face-DOWN, so it simply **can't activate** — not a "backfire," just no legal target. Hold for after a flip if at all. The card Asta.PIXEA actually punishes is **Dark Magical Circle's banish** (and Bystial). |
| **Dimension Shifter** (they run 3) | **THE engine-killer** | Banishes everything that would hit your GY for a turn → your **Eternal Soul recursion stops working.** Ash and Called don't stop it. Under Shifter, play from hand + field only. |
| **Mulcharmy Fuwalos** (2) | weak here | Enneacraft is low-summon control — few Special Summons to trigger it. It's your going-second tool for the *combo* matchups, not this grind. (Replaces the cut Nibiru, which was likewise dead here.) |
| **Called by the Grave** | hold it | save for **their** Maxx "C" on your turn so you can develop. |

**How DM breaks the board:** their wall is **face-down Defense + Pendulum-zone Enneacrafts** → **non-targeting / "unaffected" beats it.**
- **Eye/Gaze → Timaeus the United** = an **unaffected** body that walks straight through Atil/Asta's negates. Premium line here.
- **Eye/Gaze → Master of Chaos → banish-all** clears the face-down wall *without flipping it* (banishing face-downs triggers no FLIP effects, no E.C. Counters). Costs tributing a LIGHT + a DARK — **DMG the Magician's Apprentice is your only LIGHT body.** (Verified 2026-06-14: Eye of Timaeus lists "Dark Magician" as material, so one DM makes Master of Chaos.)
- **Kill Enneapolis with Dark Magic Attack** (now mained) — Enneapolis is the 9-counter burn engine, and their Pendulum-zone Enneacrafts sit in the S/T zone, so DMA wipes the whole counter-banking half at once.
- **Dark Magical Circle's banish IS targeting** → can be eaten by Asta.PIXEA. Use Circle's *reveal/early* value, not the banish, into a set board.
- **Bait the flips:** their disruption only triggers in response to your action — make a cheap, expendable activation first to spend their negate, *then* commit Circle / Eternal Soul. Play **sequentially**, one threat at a time, so each negate is spent individually (Atil punishes stacked chains).

**Win condition:** **race the burn, don't out-grind it.** 9 counters takes several turns; DM has the bigger bodies (2500 DM, 3200 Dragon Knight, Timaeus). Pressure life points and **close before ~turn 6.** They have no hand-trap-proof "I win" board — just a grindy wall. Eternal Soul (continuous, dodges flip-negates that key off *activations*) is your engine through it; protect it with DM the Dragon Knight.

**One-liner:** *"It's Enneacraft — Tier-3 face-down Flip/Pendulum burn-control. Hold Ash (and Droll if teched) for its search, DON'T fire Imperm/Veiler into its set monsters, break the wall with Timaeus-the-United (unaffected) and non-targeting removal, and race the 9-counter burn with big DM swings before turn 6."*

## vs HERO (Vision/Masked HERO → Dark Law)
Opp role: **"Trending" (~2.5), below T3** — a consistent rogue ladder deck, which is why you keep hitting it.

**The current build:** Vision HERO **Faris + Vyon** consistency engine + Destiny HERO **Malicious** extension + Elemental HERO **Shadow Mist + A Hero Lives** as the Mask Change enabler, ending on **Masked HERO Dark Law** turn 1. (Bystial HERO is NOT the current MD build — dismiss it.) Sourced lists: KJG Master I 2026-04-05; Pelincha Master I 2026-03-26 (masterduelmeta top-decks). Exact June ratios = no data (the shell is stable Mar–Jun).

**Off one card:** **A Hero Lives → SS Shadow Mist → add Mask Change from deck → Mask Change the Shadow Mist (DARK) → SS Masked HERO Dark Law.** Shadow Mist hitting GY then adds another HERO. End: Dark Law + a HERO in hand. (Faris/Vyon/Stratos/E-Emergency Call all also reach this.)

**Why it crushes DM specifically (verbatim):**
- **Masked HERO Dark Law** (Fusion, DARK, 2400): *"Any card sent to your opponent's GY is banished instead. Once per turn, if your opponent adds a card(s) from their Deck to their hand...: banish 1 random card from your opponent's hand."* → (a) **guts Eternal Soul recursion** (your GY-bound cards get banished, not sent to GY) and (b) **hand-rips you every time you search.**
- **Mask Change** (Quick-Play): sends a HERO you control → SS a same-Attribute Masked HERO from Extra. Hard to interrupt.
- **NEW 2026-06-14 — a SECOND boss: Destroyer Phoenix Enforcer (DPE)** via Fusion Destiny. Its Quick Effect *"destroy both 1 card you control and 1 card on the field"* is **non-targeting** and kills **Eternal Soul** — and when Soul leaves the field, *all your monsters are destroyed*, so your DM dies with it. This is harder to answer than Dark Law. **Dark Cavalry does NOT stop it (it only negates targeting); only Timaeus the United — "unaffected by other cards' effects" — survives it.** Don't let Eternal Soul sit on board facing a live DPE; have the Timaeus line ready.

**What lands vs you / what whiffs:**
| Your card | Lands? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Ash Blossom** | **LANDS (best)** | Ash on **A Hero Lives or Shadow Mist** denies the Mask Change tutor and stops Dark Law cold. Highest-value interaction. |
| **Infinite Impermanence** | situational | Negate **Faris or Vyon** on summon to cut the engine; does NOT undo an already-resolved Dark Law (continuous applied effect). |
| **Maxx "C"** | tax | HERO SS a lot; but they main Ash + Called to answer your Maxx C. |
| **Mulcharmy Fuwalos** (2) | ok going 2nd | HERO Special Summons through the Mask Change / Malicious lines, so on the *draw* Fuwalos nets cards; **dead on the play.** (Replaces the cut Nibiru — the Dark Law line is only ~2-3 summons, so Nibiru rarely fired in time.) |
| **Called by the Grave** | awkward here | HERO's threats are field floodgates, not GY effects — side it toward GY decks; lead on Ash/Imperm vs HERO. |
| **Super Poly** | **LANDS** | *"neither player can respond"* — ignores Dark Law/Faris; steal a HERO. **Material catch below.** |

**How DM breaks Dark Law:**
- **Dark Magical Circle is the clean out** — summon any Dark Magician with Circle up → **banish Dark Law** (banish dodges its lack of protection; doesn't target the GY). Build around this.
- **Eye/Gaze → Timaeus the United** — *unaffected by other cards' effects* — a body Dark Law can't stop, and DM the Dragon Knight shields your backrow.
- **Super Poly target math:** the easy generic **Mudragon** needs *"same Attribute, DIFFERENT Types"* — two of their HEROs are mostly DARK **Warrior**, so they collide on Type and FAIL Mudragon. The working combo is **your DARK Spellcaster (Dark Magician / Apprentice Illusion Magician) + their DARK HERO** = same Attribute (DARK), different Type (Spellcaster vs Warrior) → **valid Mudragon**, eating a HERO.
- **Eternal Soul caveat (inference, verify in-client):** Eternal Soul's "unaffected" protects your **Dark Magician monster**, but does **NOT** stop Dark Law's GY-replacement (that's a replacement effect on the *sent-to-GY event*, not on your monster). So Eternal Soul keeps DM alive but does **not** unlock your GY while Dark Law is up.

**Win condition:** **strip Dark Law, then grind.** Once it's off the field HERO has no floodgate and a lone 2400 body — your Eternal Soul recursion out-grinds them. Don't carelessly search into a live Dark Law (you feed the hand-rip); develop a DM with **Circle up**, banish Dark Law on summon, *then* resume the engine.

**One-liner:** *"It's Vision-HERO-into-Dark-Law. Ash their A Hero Lives / Shadow Mist to stop Dark Law landing; if it lands, banish it with Dark Magical Circle on a DM summon (or ignore it with Timaeus-the-United), use Super Poly = your DARK Spellcaster + their DARK HERO into Mudragon, then out-grind the lone 2400."*
